The Ligatures Special Interest Group invites you to Porto for the third edition of the Ligatures Summer School. Join us from July 15–17, 2026, for three days dedicated to exploring the intersection of traditional graphic heritage and emerging technological practices.

Call for Communications

Deadline: June 30, 2026
Notification of Acceptance: July 3, 2026

We invite researchers, designers, and students (graduate and post-graduate) to submit proposals for the Ligatures Summer School 2026 academic track, taking place on July 17, 2026. Research into how technological tools and traditional material practices inform visual communication, pedagogy, and design research are welcome. And especially how transitions between practices, culture and perceptions are occurring.

Transitions explores the cultural and technological
realignments defining our era.

As we navigate the accelerating shift from human-centric craftsmanship to AI-driven computational systems, the very mechanics of communication are transforming. Yet, this threshold is not an erasure—it is a vital space for the reconstruction and reinterpretation of our cultural legacies.

Tracing the continuous journey of reading and writing from their tangible, material roots to their complex, intellectual futures, Transitions aims to reflect on how we preserve, adapt, and reinvent our visual heritage. It is a platform to interrogate the shifting power dynamics of contemporary culture and to actively design the structural and typographic literacies of tomorrow.

We are accepting Extended Abstracts (including necessary visual documentation and images) for 5-minute, 15-slide presentations during the event. Authors are encouraged to submit proposals specific for this topic or in relevant topics within the Ligatures SIG mission.

Selected authors will be invited to expand their communications into full papers for a peer-reviewed digital publication hosted on the U.Porto OJS platform (expected December 2026).

Submission Categories

  • Literature Reviews: Critical mappings of historical or contemporary typographic and design landscapes.
  • Research & Development Projects: Technical, pedagogical, or applied research bridging analog and digital workflows.
  • Experimental & Speculative Design:Theoretical propositions or practical/visual modalities challenging the boundaries of letterforms and communication.
